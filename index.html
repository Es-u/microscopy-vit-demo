AI for Microscopy
Image Analysis

Investigating weakly supervised and self-supervised Vision Transformer approaches for scientific image analysis — with minimal manual annotation requirements.

Sachintha Uwanpriya | MSc Data Science & AI | University of Sri Jayewardenepura
01 / Motivation

Why This Research?

Scientific laboratories produce thousands of microscopy images that require significant expert effort to interpret, measure, and analyse. Traditional deep learning approaches demand extensive hand-annotated datasets — which are expensive and slow to create.

Annotation Cost

Pixel-level labels for microscopy data require domain expertise and hours of manual effort per image.

Scale Problem

Modern instruments can generate hundreds of images per experiment session, far outpacing manual review capacity.

Current Limitations

  • Evaluated primarily on public/synthetic datasets; expert biological validation is required.
  • Pixel measurements are in raw units and not yet calibrated to physical microscopy scales (nm/µm).
  • These represent early feasibility studies rather than production-grade lab software.
03 / Research Directions

Where This Research Could Go

Weakly Supervised Segmentation

Reducing annotation requirements through point-prompt supervision, image-level labels, and pseudo-label generation.

  • Point annotation instead of dense pixel masks.
  • Self-training using high-confidence pseudo-labels.

Laboratory Automation Integration

Building AI systems that go beyond standard image analysis to seamlessly support broader scientific workflows.

  • Automated quantification directly from instrument outputs.
  • AI-assisted report generation structuring raw experimental data.

Phased Implementation Plan
